Rexburg Today

Welcome to Rexburg, Idaho; America’s Family Community. Rexburg is a great place to work, live and raise a family. A vibrant, cultural scene filled with music, dance, activities, and community gatherings characterizes

Rexburg’s family-friendly atmosphere. Once a year, Main Street stops to host the Idaho International Dance Festival, attracting competitors from across the world. Rexburg is home to many parks and family activity centers that make it wonderful for all ages.

Interesting Information and Demographics

  • Rexburg has the youngest demographic of any city in America.
  • Brigham Young University-Idaho, Idaho’s largest university, calls Rexburg home.
  • Rexburg's public school system ranks among the best in the state.
  • Rexburg is Idaho's safest city and ranks within the top 100 safest cities in America.

History of Rexburg

The city of Rexburg was founded in March 1883 when an intrepid group of pioneers decided to settle along the banks of the Teton River. They had been sent by church leaders in Salt Lake City, under the direction of Thomas E. Ricks, to settle the Upper Snake River Valley.

The new community, named Rexburg after their leader (Ricks being German for Rex or King) quickly attracted men and women eager to bring industry and civilization to the West. By the time Idaho entered the Union as the 43rd state in 1890, Rexburg was the second largest city in the state.

Teton Dam Flood

As part of the History of the town we need to mention the Teton Dam Flood. Located in the eastern part of the state, between Fremont and Madison counties, the Teton Dam suffered a catastrophic failure on June 5, 1976, as it was filling for the first time.

The collapse of the dam resulted in the deaths of eleven people and 16,000 livestock.The dam cost about $100 million to build and the federal government paid over $300 million in claims related to its failure. Total damage estimates have ranged up to $2 billion. The dam has not been rebuilt.

Rexburg Rapids

Rexburg Rapids is a premium outdoor water park featuring slides, a climbing wall, and a lazy river. There is a kids park as well which offers plenty of fun for the whole family. It has been in operation since 2011 and offers the perfect place to cool off on a hot summer day.

Rexburg Idaho Temple

The Rexburg Idaho Temple was the third temple built in Idaho, following the Idaho Falls Idaho Temple (1945) and the Boise Idaho Temple (1984).

The announcement of the Rexburg Idaho Temple came three and a half years after the announcement that two-year Ricks College would become four-year Brigham Young University–Idaho.

The Rexburg Idaho Temple stands adjacent to Church-owned Brigham Young University–Idaho. The Church's other two universities; Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah, and Brigham Young University–Hawaii in Laie, Hawaii; also have adjacent temples.
